# Idea: Personal Finance Task Tracker

## Problem Statement
Managing personal finances is fragmented — people juggle spreadsheets, banking apps, and mental notes to track bills, savings goals, and spending tasks. There is no simple, task-oriented tool that treats financial actions (pay bill, transfer savings, review budget) as actionable to-dos with deadlines and priorities, leading to missed payments, forgotten goals, and financial stress.

## Target Users
- **Primary:** Individuals aged 20–45 managing personal budgets, recurring bills, and savings goals
- **Secondary:** Freelancers and gig workers tracking irregular income and variable expenses
- **Scale:** Personal/single-user app
- **Frequency:** Daily or weekly check-ins to review and complete financial tasks

## Proposed Solution
A task-list app purpose-built for personal finance where each to-do item is a financial action — pay a bill, set aside savings, review a subscription, etc. Tasks include due dates, amounts, categories (bills, savings, debt, income), and completion status. Users get a clear view of what financial actions need to be taken and when, without requiring bank integrations or complex budgeting setups.

## Success Metrics
- Metric 1: Users complete ≥80% of their scheduled financial tasks on time within the first month of use
- Metric 2: Reduce missed bill payments to 0 for active users within 60 days of adoption
- Metric 3: Daily active usage of ≥3 days/week after the first 2 weeks of onboarding

## Risks & Assumptions
- Risk 1: Low user retention if task entry feels too manual — likelihood: High. Mitigation: recurring task templates and smart defaults
- Risk 2: Users may expect bank/account syncing and feel the app is too limited — likelihood: Medium. Mitigation: clear scope messaging; plan sync as a future phase
- Assumption 1: Users are willing to manually log financial tasks in exchange for simplicity and focus
- Assumption 2: A task-list UX (vs. dashboard/chart-heavy UX) is sufficient for the target user's daily finance management needs
